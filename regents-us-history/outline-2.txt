## Nationalism + Sectionalism

War of 1812- 
	War between U.S. and Britain over trade in the Atlantic Ocean.
War Hawk position- 
	going to war would be a good idea
name of treaty
	the Treaty of Ghent.
major military effects
	there really were no major military changes. No side won anything significant.
primary effect
	a rise in American nationalism (intense pride for one’s nation or culture).

Monroe Doctrine
	a foreign policy that discouraged Europe from further colonizing Latin America, so that their influence in the Western Hemisphere would be reduced; Roosevelt Corollary to Monroe Doctrine: the Monroe Doctrine permitted the U.S. to intervene actively in the affairs of Latin American nations.

Andrew Jackson-- why did he win?
	Since property limitations on voting were abolished, lower-class people influenced the election more

	politicians giving government positions to their supporters as rewards

## Westward Expansion

Gold Rush
	the wave of migration into California and Western Territories due to the discovery of gold
Homestead Act definition-
	Gave free land to settlers on the condition that they would occupy it for a number of years
Homestead Act geography-
	settling of the Great Plains
Pacific Railway Act
	gave the railroad companies cheap land in order to support the building of the transcontinental railroad

Manifest Destiny
	the philosophy that it was right for the US to conquer more land, justifying westward expansion and annexing overseas territories

	Harriet Tubman (Underground Railroad)
	Harriet Beecher Stowe (Uncle Tom's Cabin)

Texas annexation controversy
	the North didn't want Texas to be added as a slave state

Acts to try to compromise over slavery's spread [LIST 3]
	Missouri Compromise of 1820
	Compromise of 1850
	Kansas-Nebraska Act (1854): Popular Sovereignty; whether slavery should exist in a new territory should be decided by the settlers, not by the federal government

Dred Scott Supreme Court case (1857)-
	ruled that Congress could not ban slavery anywhere
Why was slavery more widespread in the South?
	plantation system + cotton production technology
Bleeding Kansas-
	Phrase used to describe clashes between proslavery and antislavery groups intensified by popular sovereignty legislation
