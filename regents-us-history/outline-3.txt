## U.S. Civil War

Lincoln's expressed goal at the beginning			preserve the Union

restrictions on rights during wartime [LIST 2]

habeas corpus was suspended during the civil war to jail anti-Unionists without giving a reason.
censorship of anti-Union newspapers

Emancipation Proclamation-			declared that all Confederate slaves needed to be freed, a tactical trick to get England to not side with the South

Reasons for North (Union) Victory			stronger economy and better manufacturing facilities

Economic result of Civil War [LIST 2]

the North economy prospered due to gov't demand for war goods (guns, supplies, transportation)
the South economy was so bad; carpetbaggers were people from the North moving to the South to buy up land at cratered prices

## Post-Civil War/ Reconstruction Era

Reconstruction Era-			Time period following the Civil War, when the Southern States were reorganized and reintegrated back into the Union.
economic goals for the New South			industrial development + agricultural diversification (i.e., industrialization + growing more than cash crops)
Lincoln's Plan for reconstruction			Wanted to give out lots of amnesty.
Radical Republicans-			Group of Republicans that wanted harsh punishments instead of Lincoln's approach
Lincoln is Assassinated-who takes over?			President Andrew Johnson

Causes of Johnson's impeachment [LIST 2]

He disagreed with the Radical Republicans (i.e., the Reconstruction controversy).
He fired the Secretary of War, Edwin Stanton, without consulting the Senate

13th Amendment-			formally abolished slavery in the U.S.
14th Amendment-			citizenship to African Americans and legally protected them under the Bill of Rights and U.S. Constitution.
15th Amendment-			African Americans voting rights. The amendment had limited practical effect because Southern States kept using poll taxes and literacy tests.

List of ways African American rights were limited [LIST 5]

Jim Crow Laws
Black Codes
Ku Klux Klan
"separate but equal" (Plessy v. Ferguson 1896)
Sharecropping: a lack of land ownership, therefore never really getting an economic independence (i.e., after the Civil War, many freed slaves ended up subordinate to a farm owner, who either leased land or had people work for the owner (sharecropper)).

Jim Crow Laws would not be ended until the passage of the 			Civil Rights Act 1964.

## Industrialization/Industrial Revolution

Causes of Industrial Revolution [LIST 3]

Erie Canal and transcontinental railroad
federal subsidies of railroads after the Civil War
mechanization of agriculture

During the Industrial Revolution, unprecedented amounts of what kinds of workers? [LIST 2]

child labor
immigrants

What was the social structure of Industrial Revolution?			severe income inequality in cities

What was the key advantage of the corporate system?			ability to raise lots of capital with limited risk to the investors

How did Big Business cut down on competition? [LIST 4]

tariff (i.e, tariffs that favor domestic companies over, say, European companies)
monopolies, trusts, and other Robber Barron tactics (e.g., Standard Oil Trust, John D. Rockefeller, J.P. Morgan, Cornelius Vanderbilt)
division of labor (disfavors small businesses)

What did social darwinism justify?			virtually everything from monopolies and trusts to income inequality and foreign expansion

What is the central dichotomy of Free Enterprise?			bad government intervention vs. natural fluctuation of supply-demand and prices
Free Enterprise is also known as			Laissez-Faire
